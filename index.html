Dr. Manil Maskey

I am a Senior Research Scientist and Project Manager at NASA, with a passion for data science, artificial intelligence, and Earth science. I hold a B.S. degree in Computer Science and Mathematics, as well as M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science, where my research focused on high dimensional data visualization, computer vision, and machine learning.

With over 24+ years of professional experience, my career has been diverse and exciting. I started as a Software Engineer at the National Cash Registry/Teradata, working on data mining solutions for customer relationship management. Later, I spent 12 years as a research scientist at UAH, developing innovative machine learning solutions to tackle challenging problems in Earth science.

My journey led me to NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center (MSFC), where I played a key role in modernizing data center systems and transitioning to the commercial cloud environment. As the manager of one of the NASA's Distributed Active Archive Centers—Global Hydrometeorology Resource Center, I was responsible for its research and operations. Simultaneously, I helped establish a data science team within the IMPACT project, which focuses on advanced artificial intelligence tools and applications for Earth science and other domains.

Over the years, I have managed competitive programs for NASA's Earth Science Data Systems program, including leading the Advancing Collaborative Connections for Earth System Science (ACCESS) solicitation and established the Commercial Smallsat Data Acquisition (CSDA) program’s data management project at MSFC.

In 2019, I began a NASA Headquarter detail to support the Science Mission Directorate's (SMD) Open Source Science Initiative. As the Data Science and Innovation Lead, I have the honor of leading SMD's AI initiative in close collaboration with all NASA science divisions. This initiative is a testament to our commitment to advancing science with AI, fostering innovation, collaboration, and transparency across the scientific community.

Currently, I am also leading the development of the US Government Greenhouse Gas Center Information and Monitoring System, building upon the NASA Visualization, Exploration, and Data Analysis (VEDA) project, which I conceptualized and initiated.

In addition to my work at NASA, I am an adjunct faculty member at the University of Alabama in Huntsville (UAH) Atmospheric Science department. I hold leadership positions in various organizations, including being a senior member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) and serving as the chair of the IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Society (GRSS) Earth Science Informatics Technical Committee. I am also a member of the American Geophysical Union (AGU) and AGU Fall Meeting Planning Committee, European Geosciences Union (EGU), and Association for Advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AAAI).
